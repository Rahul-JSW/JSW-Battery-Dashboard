Module 02 · UBC & UBE Estimation

Usable Battery Capacity & Energy

Monte Carlo simulation of pack-level Usable Battery Capacity (UBC) and Usable Battery Energy (UBE), accounting for cell-to-cell capacity spread, SoC spread, and multi-pack voltage balancing losses.

Definitions
What are UBC and UBE?

UBC (Usable Battery Capacity) is the net Ah a battery pack can deliver between its high-SoC and low-SoC limits, after accounting for cell-level imbalance and voltage-balancing constraints imposed by series connection. It is always less than the simple nominal capacity × SoC window estimate.

UBE (Usable Battery Energy) converts UBC into Wh/kWh by multiplying by the pack voltage (series cells × average OCV at 50% SoC × parallel strings × number of packs). UBE is what ultimately determines vehicle range or system runtime.

Loss Mechanisms
Why is True UBC < Traditional UBC?
1
Capacity spread
Series cells are limited by the weakest cell; manufacturing variation means no pack reaches its average capacity.
2
SoC spread
Cells within a pack start at slightly different SoC values; the top-of-charge equalisation removes usable Ah from higher-SoC cells.
3
Multi-pack voltage balancing
Packs connected in parallel must reach the same terminal voltage; the weakest pack forces others to stop earlier, reducing usable capacity further.
